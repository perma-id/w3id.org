Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to university entries from those students which are enrolling for the first time in each official degree.

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to university entries. The data used in it comes from a set of public spanish universities, more specifically, they belong to: the Automonous University of Madrid (UAM), the Carlos III University of Madrid (UC3M), the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), the King Juan Carlos University (URJC), the University of Valladolid (UVa) and the University of Huelva (UHU). In this way, this vocabulary describes information about students, that come from a high school center, which enroll for the first time in a university, enrolling in a given academic year. Furthermore, the university has a center and a set of degrees.

Moreover, it uses the geo and geosparql vocabularies to represent the geographical location of the center and the university associated to each entry, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. Additionally, it reuses terms from the schema.org vocabulary for the representation of concepts and relations that are used to describe people. Finally, it also reuses the organizations vocabulary to represent the center and the university entities, which gathers all the entries that exist in it.

Vocabulary for the representation of university entries: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of university entries:

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from university entries. To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the Estudiante class, which collects information about the admission score obtained to enter university, his date of birth, the studies speciality type taken to enter university, the end of studies year, if the student enters the spanish university system (SUE) for the first time, if he enrolls in the first year and if he is taking any special course. Furthermore, the Estudiante class has several SKOS concept schemes to represent the field of study he chose to study in order to enter university, the speciality type of it, his admission form, his dedication, his gender, his occupation as well as his father's and mother's occupation and also their level of studies and if he belongs to a large family. Besides, the following student information is registered for each entry: his nationality, residence and end of studies country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries, and the municipality (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), province and autonomous community of the high school center where the student studied. Additionally, it has been reused the vocabulary schema:Person to describe the student. Finally, it has been also reused the vocabulary of org:Organization to show how the class Universidad has a suborganization, which is the Centro class. In this way, this class has been reused to explain that both mentioned classes (Universidad and Centro) have a geographical location, represented by the vocabulary geosparql.
