Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to university Teaching and Research Staff (PDI).

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to university Teaching and Research Staff (PDI). The data used in it comes from a set of public spanish universities, more specifically, they belong to: the Automonous University of Madrid (UAM), the Carlos III University of Madrid (UC3M), the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), the King Juan Carlos University (URJC), the University of Valladolid (UVa) and the University of Huelva (UHU). In this way, this vocabulary describes information about the PDI, which works in a university, belonging to a responsible unit as well as a specific knowledge area.

Moreover, it uses the geo and geosparql vocabularies to represent the geographical location of the university where the PDI carries out his work, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. Additionally, it reuses terms from the schema.org vocabulary for the representation of concepts and relations that are used to describe people. Finally, it also reuses the organizations vocabulary to represent the university entity, which gathers all the Teaching and Research Staff (PDI) that work in it.

Vocabulary for the representation of university Teaching and Research Staff (PDI): Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of university Teaching and Research Staff (PDI):

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from university Teaching and Research Staff (PDI). To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the PersonalDocenteInvestigador class, which collects information about his date of birth, his year of incorporation, his year of incorporation to the public administration (AP), his year of incorporation to the teaching staff, the year in which the PDI got his doctorate degree (in case he has one), his year of thesis lecture, his number of weekly hours with a part-time job, if his job is paid or not, if he has a european honorable mention or not, if he has been the main researcher in a project, his number of three year periods, his number of five year periods, his number of six year periods and his number of thesis written. Furthermore, the PersonalDocenteInvestigador class has several SKOS concept schemes to represent his gender, his dedication, his contract type, the staff type, the doctorate degree, the category of the scale corp and his administrative situation. Besides, it is described for each PDI his nationality country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries, and also his residence municipality (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), province and autonomous community. Regarding the other classes, the PDI works in a university being part of a specific knowledge area during a given year, as well as he also belongs to a responsible unit, for example, a department. Moreover, if the PDI studied a doctorate degree, information about the doctorate university is also collected, such as the nationality of the country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries where the university is located in. Additionally, it has been reused the vocabulary schema:Person to describe the PDI. Finally, it has been also reused the vocabulary of org:Organization to show how the classes Universidad and UniversidadDoctorado (in case it exists) have a geographical location, represented by the vocabulary geosparql.
