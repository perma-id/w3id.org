# Historic Survey Ontology (HSV)

The **Historic Survey Ontology (HSV)** provides a formal knowledge representation for the activities, outputs, and actors involved in the survey of historic buildings and urban heritage.

## Namespace

```
https://w3id.org/hsv#
```

## Description

HSV is organised around two main axes:

- **Spatial organisation** — a hierarchy of classes (Region, Historic Centre, Aggregate, Structural Unit, Facade) reflecting the nested scales at which historic buildings and urban fabrics are typically documented
- **Documents and stakeholders** — a central `hsv:Document` class linking heterogeneous survey outputs (point clouds, drawings, photos, publications, BIM models) to the spatial entities they characterise and to the actors involved in their production (inspectors, post-processers, authors)

The ontology also captures contextual knowledge relevant to heritage interpretation, including traditional construction techniques and seismic zonation.
